Journey Timeline

Follow the chronological journey of Saloo & Neena Chowdhury's record-breaking adventures across six continents.

1989

First Circumnavigation Attempt

Saloo and Neena embark on their first journey to circumnavigate the globe by car, covering 45,000 kilometres in 69 days, 19 hours and 5 minutes.

Starting from Delhi, India

Key Achievements:

  • First circumnavigation by car
  • Covered six continents
  • Guinness World Record certified
1991

British Army Challenge

The British Army accomplishes circumnavigation in 40 days, leading Guinness to drop the "first" title from the Chowdhurys' record.

Global

Key Achievements:

  • Army had significant advantages
  • No customs or visa delays
  • Royal Air Force backup
1991

Record Breaking Journey

Determined to reclaim their title, the Chowdhurys set off again on November 7, 1991, covering 40,535 kilometres in just 39 days, 20 hours, 15 minutes.

Starting from Delhi, India

Key Achievements:

  • Beat British Army by 3 hours 45 minutes
  • New world record
  • Fastest circumnavigation
1993-1998

Legal Battle

Five-year legal battle with Guinness Publishing Limited in Calcutta High Court to be recognized as both first and fastest circumnavigators.

Calcutta High Court, India

Key Achievements:

  • Filed case for recognition
  • Demanded £7.5 million damages
  • Fought for five years
1998

Victory and Recognition

On April 7, 1998, Guinness Publishing Limited signs MOU recognizing the Chowdhurys as first and fastest circumnavigators.

Legal Settlement

Key Achievements:

  • Official recognition restored
  • Choudhury Trophy named in their honor
  • Legacy secured for Kolkata
Present

Continuing Legacy

The Chowdhurys continue to inspire adventurers worldwide, with their eyes set on bigger targets and new challenges.

Kolkata, India

Key Achievements:

  • Guinness World Records Road Challenge
  • Inspiration for future adventurers
  • Kolkata on world map
